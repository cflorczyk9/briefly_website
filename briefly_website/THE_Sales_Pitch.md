# The Sales Pitch

## The Problem

A client with $2M passes away, and suddenly you don't have one $2M relationship. You have three or four at $500-700k each. The assets stay on the books, but the work triples. Three times the meetings, three times the follow-ups, three times the personal details you need to keep straight. Most practices aren't set up for that.

## Proof I Understand the Workflow

I spent three years on a team managing over $4 billion at Morgan Stanley. A big part of my job was digging through old emails, notes, and account statements before every client meeting so the advisors walked in prepared. I was paid over six figures to basically do that. It works fine when you have 50 relationships, but at 150, it falls apart.

## The Product

Briefly takes the documents you're already working with (statements, notes, emails) and builds a living profile of each client relationship. Before a meeting, you get a brief that pulls together what actually matters: portfolio changes worth flagging, life events they've mentioned, open items from last time. There's no new software to learn and no CRM migration. It just reads what you already have.

## Compounding Value

Every meeting you log and every note you add, the system builds a deeper picture of that client. Six months in, it knows the relationship almost as well as you do. A year in, it starts catching things you might miss, like a client bringing up cash flow concerns across three separate conversations.

## Close

The point isn't to replace the human side of advising. It's the opposite. The less time you spend digging through files to prepare, the more time you actually spend advising. Your clients notice when you remember the details. They definitely notice when you don't.
